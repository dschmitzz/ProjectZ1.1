Prime Factorization

A prime number is an integer greater than 1 that has no factors other than 1 and itself.

The set of prime numbers is {2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, ...}

The fundamental theorem of arithmetic states that every positive integer has a unique prime factorization.

For example, the prime factorization of 9 is 3*3 or 3^2.

Pairwise Relatively Prime

Two integers are relatively prime, or coprime, if the only positive integer that divides both of them is 1.

For example, 16 and 21 are coprime.
