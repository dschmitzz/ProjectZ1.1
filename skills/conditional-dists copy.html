Conditional Distributions

Conditional Mean & Variance

The conditional mean of \(Y\) given \(X = x\), $$E(Y|x) = \mu_{Y|x} = \int_y yf_{Y|x}(y)$$

The conditional variance of \(Y\) given \(X = x\), $$V(Y|x) = \sigma_{Y|x}^2 = \int_y (y - \mu_{Y|x})^2 f_{Y|x}(y) = \int_y y^2 f_{Y|x}(y) - \mu_{Y|x}^2$$

Properties of Independence

For random variables \(X\) and \(Y\), the statement that \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent is logically equivalent to the following statements:

  1. \(f_{XY}(x,y) = f_X(x)f_Y(y)\) for all \(x\), \(y\)
  2. \(f_{Y|x}(y) = f_Y(y)\) for all \(x\), \(y\) with \(f_X(x)>0\)
  3. \(f_{X|y}(x) = f_X(x)\) for all \(x\), \(y\) with \(f_Y(y)>0\)
  4. \(P(X \in A, Y \in B) = P(X \in A)P(Y \in B) \) for any sets \(A \in X\) and \(B \in Y\).
