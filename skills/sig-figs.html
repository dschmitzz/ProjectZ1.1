Significant Figures

In natural science, it is necessary to evaluate the significance of measured results.

Measured digits can be broken into five categories:

  1. Non-zero measurable digits are significant.
  2. \(0\)s between non-zero measurable digits are significant.
  3. Trailing measurable \(0\)s are significant.
  4. Leading \(0\)s are non-significant.
  5. Digits beyond the precision of the instrument used to measure them are non-significant.
