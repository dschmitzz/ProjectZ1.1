Propositions

Proposition

A proposition is a statement with a value of either true or false.

Propositions can be represented by propositional variables, which are conventionally letters of the sequence \(p, q, r, s, ...\)

Every proposition has a truth value—\(T\) if it is true, \(F\) if it is false.

Types of Proposition

An atomic proposition is one that cannot be expressed in terms of simpler propositions.

A compound proposition is formed by combining two or more atomic propositions with logic operators.
