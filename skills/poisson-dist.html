Poisson Distribution

Poisson Process

Poisson process: Used to model how long until the next occurence of an event, typically natural disasters and such. Consists of small intervals \(\Delta t\). As \(\Delta t\) approaches 0,

  1. The probability of more than one event in the subinterval tends to 0.
  2. The probability of one event in the subinterval tends to \(\lambda \Delta t\).
  3. Events within subintervals are independent of events in other subintervals.

Poisson Distribution

Random variable \(X\): # of events in a Poisson process

Given \(0 \lt \lambda \) and \(T\) as the length,

probability mass function: \(P(X=x) = f(x) = e^{-\lambda T} {(\lambda T)^x \over x!}\)

\(x = 0, 1, 2, ...\)

Poisson Mean & Variance

Given a Poisson random variable \(X\) over interval of length \(T\) with parameter \(\lambda\):

mean, \(\mu = E(X) = \lambda T\)

variance, \(\sigma^2 = V(X) = \lambda T\)
