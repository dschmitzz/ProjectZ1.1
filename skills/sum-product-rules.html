Sum & Product Rules

Sum Rule

If there are \(m\) ways to do something and \(n\) ways of doing another thing, and you cannot do both at the same time, then there are \(m + n\) ways to choose one.

Product Rule

If a procedure consists of two steps, independent of each other, with \(m\) possible results of the first step and \(n\) possible results of the second, then the total procedure can be performed in \(mn\) ways.
