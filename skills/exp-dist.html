Exponential Distribution

Poisson Process

Poisson process: Consists of small intervals \(\Delta t\). As \(\Delta t\) tends to 0,

1. The probability of more than one event in the subinterval tends to 0.

2. The probability of one event in the subinterval tends to \(\lambda \Delta t\).

3. Events within subintervals are independent of those in other subintervals.

Exponential Distribution

Random variable \(X\): distance between successive events from a Poisson process with mean number of events \(\lambda > 0\)

probability density function, \(f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}\)

for \(0 \leq x \lt \infty\)

Exponential Mean & Variance

Given an exponential random variable X with parameter \(\lambda\):

mean, \(\mu\ = E(X) = {1 \over \lambda}\)

variance, \(\sigma^2 = V(X) = {1 \over \lambda^2}\)

Lack of Memory Property

If \(X\) is an exponential random variable, $$P(X \lt t_1 + t_2 | X > t_1) = P(X \lt t_2)$$
