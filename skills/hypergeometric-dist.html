Hypergeometric Distribution

Hypergeometric Distribution

Random variable \(X\): \(x_1, x_2, ..., x_n\) all have equal probability within range \(n\).

probability mass function: \(P(X=x) = f(x) = {\left( \begin{array}{c} K \\ x \end{array} \right)\left( \begin{array}{c} N-K \\ n-x \end{array} \right) \over \left( \begin{array}{c} N \\ n \end{array} \right)}\)

\(x\) = max\(\{0, n+K-N\}\) to min\(\{K, n\}\)

Hypergeometric Mean & Variance

Given a hypergeometric random variable \(X\) for of consecutive integers \(a, a + 1, a + 2, ..., b\) on range \(a \leq b\)

mean, \(\mu = E(X) = np\)

variance, \(\sigma^2 = V(X) = np(1 - p){N - n \over N - 1}\)
