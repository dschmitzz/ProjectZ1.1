Scientific Method

Hypotheses, Theories, Laws

A scientific fact is a simple observation in the natural world.

A hypothesis is a proposed explanation of specific observations that can be tested with experiments.

A scientific theory is a general conclusion supported by a large body of evidence that explains why or how some natural phenomenon works.

A scientific law is a statement, supported by a large body of evidence, about a relationship between facts. It predicts what will happen, but does not explain why or how.

A theorem, or axiom, is a law that is proven to be true for every possible case. It is impossible to definitively prove anything in the natural world, so theorems only exist in the formal sciences (mathematics, logic, etc.).

The Scientific Method

  1. Make an observation or ask a question.
  2. Research areas of interest.
  3. Form a hypothesis from observations, research, and assumptions.
  4. Test the hypothesis by performing repeated experiments.
  5. Analyze the data from the experiments and form conclusions.
  6. Repeat.

Experiments

Experiments are designed to test a hypothesis by adjusting one variable (leaving everything else constant) and observing the effects.

The independent variable is the factor manipulated by the researchers to elicit some response.

Dependent variables are the factors affected by the independent variable and measured by the researchers.

An experimental group is a group (of animals, objects, etc.) in which the researchers observe the effects the independent variable.

A control group is often used next to an experimental group. The researchers do nothing to this group, to see how much change in the dependent variable can be attributed to other factors.

Data Analysis

Data are recorded observations collected for scientific inquiry.

Data can be qualitative (descriptions) or quantitative (numbers).

Inductive reasoning is the process of condensing many observations into general conclusions.

Deductive reasoning is the process of predicting a specific result based on general premises.
