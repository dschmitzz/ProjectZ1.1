Binomial Distribution

Conduct a series of \(n\) Bernoulli trials (those resulting in either success or failure, independent of each other, with constant probability \(p\) of success, 0 \(\lt p \lt 1\)).

Random variable \(X\) = # of successes after \(n\) trials

probability mass function: \(P(X=x) = f(x) = \left( \begin{array}{c} n \\ x \end{array} \right)p^x(1 - p)^{n-x}\)

\(x = 0, 1, ..., n\)

Binomial Mean & Variance

Given a binomial random variable \(X\) with probability \(p\) of success and \(n\) trials:

mean, \(\mu = E(X) = np\)

variance, \(\sigma^2 = V(X) = np(1 - p)\)
