Independence

Independence of two events

The statement that two events are independent is logically equivalent to the following statements:

  1. \(P(A|B) = P(A)\)
  2. \(P(B|A) = P(B)\)
  3. \(P(A\cap B) = P(A)P(B)\)

Circuits

Given two nodes in series, if node \(A\) has probability of success \(P(A)\) and node \(B\) has probability of success \(P(B)\), the probability of the series circuit operating is \(P(A\cap B) = P(A)P(B)\).

Given two nodes in parallel, if node \(A\) has probability of success \(P(A)\) and node \(B\) has probability of success \(P(B)\), the probability of the parallel circuit operating is \(P(A\cup B)\) \(= P(A) + P(B) - P(A\cap B)\) \(= P(A) + P(B) - P(A)P(B)\).

To calculate the total probability of success of a circuit with series and parallel components, combine pairs of nodes into single probabilities with the methods above until you end up with two nodes in series or parallel, which you can use to get the circuit's total probability of success.
