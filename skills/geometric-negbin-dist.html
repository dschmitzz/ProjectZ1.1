Geometric & Negative Binomial Distributions

Geometric Distribution

Conduct a series of Bernoulli trials (those resulting in either success or failure, independent of each other, with constant probability \(p\) of success, \(0 \lt p \lt 1\)).

Random variable \(X\) = # of trials until the first success

probability mass function: \(P(X=x) = f(x) = (1 - p)^{x-p}p\)

\(x = r, r+1, r+2, ...\)

Geometric Mean & Variance

Given a geometric random variable \(X\) with probability \(p\) of success:

mean, \(\mu = E(X) = {1 \over p}\)

variance, \(\sigma^2 = V(X) = {1 - p \over p^2}\)

Negative Binomial Distribution

Conduct a series of Bernoulli trials (those resulting in either success or failure, independent of each other, with constant probability \(p\) of success, \(0 \lt p \lt 1\)).

Random variable \(X\) = # of trials until \(r\) successes occur

probability mass function: \(P(X = x) = f(x) = \left( \begin{array}{c} x-1 \\ r-1 \end{array} \right)(1 - p)^{x-r}p^r\)

Negative Binomial Mean & Variance

Given a negative binomial random variable X with probability p of success and r successes:

mean, \(\mu = E(X) = {r \over p}\)

variance, \(\sigma^2 = V(X) = {r(1 - p) \over p^2}\)

Note that all geometric formulas are just negative binomial formulas but plugging in 1 for \(r\), as expected!
