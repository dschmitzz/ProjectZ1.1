Logic Operators

Negation (NOT)

The negation of a proposition \(p\) is written ¬\(p\).

¬\(p\) is the opposite, or inverse, of \(p\).

Conjunction (AND)

The conjunction of propositions \(p\) and \(q\) is written \(p ∧ q\).

\(p ∧ q\) is only true if both \(p\) and \(q\) are true.

Disjunction (OR)

The disjunction (inclusive OR) of propositions \(p\) and \(q\) is written \(p ∨ q\).

\(p ∨ q\) is true unless \(p\) and \(q\) are both false.

Exclusive OR (XOR)

The exclusive OR of propositions \(p\) and \(q\) is written \(p ⊕ q\).

\(p ⊕ q\) is true only if \(p\) and \(q\) have different truth values.
